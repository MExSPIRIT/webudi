Assam

Assam is known for Assam tea and Assam silk. The state was the first site for oil drilling in Asia. Assam is home to the one-horned Indian rhinoceros, along with the wild water buffalo, pygmy hog, tiger and various species of Asiatic birds, and provides one of the last wild habitats for the Asian elephant. The State of Assam, popularly known as the land of the red river and blue hills is the gateway to the North East India. Geographically the state is extending from 22°19’ to 28°16’ North Latitude and 89°42’ to 96°30’ East Longitude between the foot hills of the Eastern Himalayas and the Patkai and Naga Hill Ranges. The state is bordered in the North by Bhutan and in the East by Arunachal Pradesh. Along the south lie Nagaland, Manipur and Mizoram. Meghalaya lies to the South-West, West Bengal and Bangladesh to the West. The State is divided into 33 administrative districts.

Arunachal Pradesh

Arunachal Pradesh, located in the northeastern part of India, is a state renowned for its breathtaking natural beauty and rich cultural diversity. Nestled in the eastern Himalayas, it shares borders with Bhutan to the west, China to the north, and Myanmar to the east. The capital city, Itanagar, is known for its vibrant culture and historical significance, featuring landmarks like the Ita Fort and the Ganga Lake. Arunachal Pradesh is inhabited by numerous indigenous tribes, each with its unique traditions, languages, and festivals. Major tribes include the Apatani, Nyishi, and Monpa, contributing to a colorful tapestry of cultural heritage. Festivals such as Losar, Solung, and Mopin reflect the state’s deep-rooted traditions and are celebrated with much enthusiasm.

Mizoram

Mizoram, a picturesque state in northeastern India, is renowned for its lush landscapes, rolling hills, and vibrant culture. Nestled between Bangladesh and Myanmar, Mizoram is characterized by its mountainous terrain, primarily composed of the Lushai Hills, which are dotted with dense forests and scenic valleys. Mizoram is home to diverse ethnic groups, with the Mizo people being the predominant community. Their rich cultural heritage is reflected in their traditional music, dance, and festivals, such as Chapchar Kut and Paite Mizo. The Mizo language, a member of the Sino-Tibetan language family, is widely spoken and serves as a unifying factor among the people.

Tripura

Tripura, a northeastern state of India, is known for its lush green landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and diverse tribal communities. Nestled between Bangladesh on its west, north, and south, it is one of the smallest states in India, with an area of approximately 10,491 square kilometers. The capital city, Agartala, is an important cultural and political center. The state is characterized by its picturesque hills, valleys, and numerous rivers, making it a haven for nature lovers. The prominent hill ranges include the Baramura and the Jampui Hills, which are home to vibrant flora and fauna. The state's climate is predominantly tropical, with a monsoon season that significantly influences its agriculture and lifestyle.

Manipur

Manipur, nestled in the northeastern part of India, is a state renowned for its rich cultural heritage and stunning natural landscapes. Often referred to as the "Jewel of India," it is bordered by Nagaland to the north, Mizoram to the south, and shares an international border with Myanmar to the east. Economically, agriculture plays a vital role, with rice being the staple crop. Handicrafts, including pottery, weaving, and bamboo work, also contribute significantly to the local economy. Manipur is also known for its martial arts, particularly Thang-Ta and Huyel Langlon, which reflect the state’s historical significance and valor.

Meghalaya

Meghalaya, a northeastern state of India, is renowned for its stunning landscapes, rich culture, and unique biodiversity. Nestled between Assam and Bangladesh, it is often referred to as the "Abode of Clouds," owing to its high altitude and frequent rainfall, which contribute to lush green hills, rolling valleys, and vibrant flora. Shillong, the capital city, is often referred to as the "Scotland of the East" due to its picturesque scenery and pleasant climate. The city boasts a vibrant arts scene, historical landmarks, and a blend of colonial and indigenous architecture.

Nagaland

Nagaland, a state in northeastern India, is known for its rich cultural heritage and stunning natural beauty. Bordered by Assam to the west, Arunachal Pradesh to the north, and Myanmar to the east, it boasts a diverse topography that includes hills, valleys, and lush forests. The state is home to various indigenous tribes, each with its own unique traditions, languages, and customs, making it a vibrant mosaic of cultures. The Naga people, known for their elaborate festivals, traditional attire, and intricate crafts, play a significant role in the state's identity.
